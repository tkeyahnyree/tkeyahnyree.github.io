Money Over Here (2021)

Money Over Here allows young adults to monitor their spending, track saving goals and learn more about financial literacy.Our platform is recommended for young adults ranging ages 18-35.

Learn About Money: Research

In wake of movements such as The Great Resignation, personal finance and wealth management are topics that have gained popularity with the help of social media. User research was conducted by ways of secondary data, interviews and competitive audit. Our target user being older Gen Z and younger Millennials, all participants were between the ages 18-32, all genders and currently a part of the workforce in any industry.

Build a Budget: Wireframing

The goal was to engage users with a modern design that houses interactive features to educate about finances and encourage good money habits.To ensure accessibility to users, we iterated sketches for a responsive web app to be viewed on web browsers or downloaded directly to a user's device.

    Usability Study

    In an unmoderated study, the low-fidelity prototypes allowed users to view a shopping budget, create a savings goal and invite friends to join the platform for rewards. From the exercises in all formats, we gathered the following insights:

    • Users want the product to be secure with personal information and learning progress.
    • Users are looking for more engaging, educational visuals.

Set a Goal: Mockups and Prototyping

Final mockups focused on iconography accompanied by descriptive text for ease of navigation and a high contrast color palette to ensure user readability of all elements.

    Takeaways

    Creating a free app for money management gives young adults the support to control their finances and begin to build wealth.

    Ongoing demand for fiscal education challenges designers and developers to create an optimal money management experience for users. In the next steps:

    • Potential workshops featuring millennial finance experts with exclusive user access.
    • Further research to ensure user needs are met within finance tools.
    • Revisit educational content for relevance and accessibility.

Salem County Animal Shelter (2021)

Updated website allows users to browse the current pet registry of a local county animal shelter that offers adoption services and set up appointments before adoption.

My Role: UX Designer, Researcher

Meet the Pets: Research

In order to create a feature where users can schedule one-on-one appointments, a competitive audit was conducted with four comparable animal shelters in neighboring counties.

The functionality goal was to ensure that users were able to view current pets available for adoption without navigating to third party sites and successfully schedule a session and if the new feature is accessible.

    User Pain Points
    • Difficulty getting in contact with shelter from home via phone and email. Wait times exceed norm or responses do not occur.
    • Competing facilities leaving pet registries outdated, causing confusion when pets of interest are no longer available.
    • Users reported most sites allow them to view pets, but encourage walk-ins for more information.
    • Busy users prefer reserved time with animals and shelter staff rather than waiting on busy days as a walk-in.

Save the Date: Wireframing

New homepage designed to acknowledge all shelter activities (Events, Employment, Education, etc.) and highlight the new scheduling feature. In an unmoderated study, 5 particpants between ages 25-45 were asked to locate Mei, the cat and schedule a meeting at the shelter.

    Usability Study

    From the scheduling prompt we gathered answers to the folllowing:

    • Is the pet registry usable?
    • Could you find the type of pet you were looking for?
    • Were you able to schedule a one-on-one?
    • Was the new feature easy or difficult to use?

Meet Mei: Mockups and Prototyping

Final designs include hierarchical design used to guide user to key actions and a color scheme of yellow, green, black and white creates high contrast for user visibility. Monthly calendar for the scheduling feature includes a plugin that allows users to tap or select the desired date, corresponding time slots are emphasized to eliminate additional scrolling or typing.

    Takeaways

    3 of 5 participants stated that they prefer reserving time over walk-ins as shelter volume fluctuates constantly.

    Users are looking for a “happy medium” when navigating sites. Animal shelter sites usually include a lot of information, the goal was to avoid being visually overwhelming but still be fun and engaging. In the next steps:

    • Consider necessity for user profiles for clinic and/or foster pet regulars to communicate with the facility online.
    • Revisit registry soon and assess any additional features to save users more time (vet records, history, etc.)
    • Strategize potential landing page layouts to keep visitors engaged and involved with events and other updates.

STY CZY (2021)

STY CZY is a merchandise app created for users to preview and purchase upcoming merchandise drops from rap artist MIR MAC. This app is tailored to hip hop culture/streetwear enthusiasts.

My Role: UX Designer, Researcher

Secure the Merch: Research

Users needed an easier way to purchase limited merchandise from their favorite musician. User research was conducted by ways of secondary data, interviews and competitive audit. Findings made us aware that streetwear or Hype-Culture is a constant trend that has only gained more popularity in recent years, its biggest issue being inaccessibility to product. Our goal was to ensure users could purchase merchandise directly on the MM app with the option of ship-to-home or in-store pickup

    User Pain Points
    • Is it in stock? Cold calling and driving to random stores tends to be unproductive.
    • Checkout Time Users are less likely to find what they need online and checkout when the app is unclear to navigate. Too many steps during the checkout process is a major issue when attempting to purchase limited product as users are often manually competing with bots.
    • Notify Me Users shared that many releases are “sprung onto them” at the last minute. If day to day routine does not permit constant access to social media, it is possible to miss entire releases.

Add to Bag: Wireframing

The challenge was to choose a layout where main functions could live without crowding the screen. Competitive audits have shown users have difficulty completing transactions during merchandise releases. To identify potential problems in browsing, selecting products, methods of receiving product and checking out that users may face, Participants of the usability study followed a prompt to purchase a sweatshirt.

    Usability Study

    Competitive audits have shown users have difficulty completing transactions during merchandise releases. We want to identify potential problems in browsing, selecting products, methods of receiving product and checking out that users may face. Participants followed the prompt to purchase a sweatshirt, where we learned the following:

    • Users want to check their bag while ordering
    • Speedy process yields positive feedback
    • Visual confirmation cues are necessary

Stay Cozy: Mockups and Prototyping

Multiple studies were conducted to ensure all elements of the app would be accessible to users. For the final product, a cool blue color palette that provides high contrast on each screen and updated main function buttons to increase readability worked best for the design.

    Takeaways

    Creating an app specifically for the musician merchandise ultimately saved users time and money by reducing or eliminating travel.

    Ongoing demand for “hype” pieces challenges designers and developers to create an optimal purchasing experience for users.. In the next steps, will look at conducting further research to ensure user needs are met during shopping experience. Investors have chosen 1 style per person-per transaction approach. Depending on demand, changes will be made to increase quantity limit per style.
